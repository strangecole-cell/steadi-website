Why We Built Steadi

Steadi was built by people who have seen firsthand how much this transition matters — and how rarely it's met with a product that actually takes it seriously.

Cole Strange, PA-C

Technical Founder

Cole spent years in emergency medicine and didn't expect the job to change him the way it did. The schedule wore down his sleep first, then everything else followed. His energy dropped, his patience shortened, his focus slipped, and he started noticing things that felt off like brain fog that wouldn't clear and a version of himself that felt harder to access. At home, with his wife and two young boys, he could feel the gap between who he wanted to be and how he was actually showing up.

What stayed with him most was that it didn't feel random. He was seeing the same kind of strain in the patients he treated every day, different stories on the surface, but underneath, the same patterns tied to disrupted sleep, chronic stress, and a body that just wasn't responding the way it used to. It forced him to look at health differently, less about isolated symptoms and more about how the body handles pressure over time.

When he stepped away from the ER, it wasn't to walk away from medicine, it was to move closer to that understanding. Perimenopause and menopause stood out quickly, not because they are simple, but because they aren't. It's a stage where so many women start to feel like their body has changed the rules overnight. Sleep becomes unpredictable, energy fades at the wrong times, weight shifts without explanation, and even thoughts and emotions can feel unfamiliar. It's frustrating, and often dismissed or oversimplified.

Steadi was built from that awareness, with respect for how real and layered this experience is, and with a focus on supporting the systems underneath it in a way that makes sense, using ingredients that have been studied and an approach that doesn't talk over women or reduce what they're going through, but instead meets them where they are.
