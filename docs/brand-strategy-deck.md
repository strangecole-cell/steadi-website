# Steadi Brand Strategy Summary Deck

## Objective

- Define Steadi's core product naming and brand architecture
- Establish behavior-based persona framework
- Map competitors (Helium 10 Xray data)
- Identify Steadi's positioning white-space

## Data Sources & Methodology

- Helium 10 Xray (Amazon ASIN revenue + reviews)
- Live Amazon PDP and review language analysis
- Heuristic behavioral personas informed by Nielsen / Mintel / Euromonitor models

---

## Competitive Landscape Key Findings

- Pragmatist / value brands dominate unit volume
- Empath-led brands (e.g., Estroven) drive multi-symptom 'relief' demand
- Researcher-led brands (DIM / I3C formulas) attract ingredient-seekers
- **Minimalist tone is under-represented → clear whitespace for Steadi**

## Strategic Takeaways for Steadi

### Cross-Brand Patterns
- Reviews skew Empath even when brands lean Researcher
- Buyers talk sleep, mood, relief more than mechanisms
- "Clinical proof" helps only when anchored in emotional outcomes
- Value brands win by setting clear time expectations
- Minimalist brands succeed with design calm, but risk seeming vague

### Implications for Steadi
- **Lead with Empath tone**, supported by Researcher proof
- **Headline = feeling outcome** ("Feel like yourself again")
- **Subtext = time-to-feel transparency** ("Most feel results by week 3")
- Use whitespace, clarity, and empathy-first language
- Mirror real user vocabulary — harvest early reviews to shape copy

### Positioning Sweet Spot
Steadi sits in the **balanced Empath–Researcher blend** zone — warm emotional headlines backed by transparent, science-grounded proof.

## Four Consumer Personas

### 1. The Researcher — "Prove it to me."
- Analytical; reads Supplement Facts; validates mechanisms and dosages
- Compares ingredients across brands; seeks certifications
- **Triggers:** Dosage transparency, clean tables/infographics, sourcing details
- **Design guidance:** Structured layout, legible typography, subtle data icons, professional palette, ingredient rationale bullets, plain-English mechanism lines

### 2. The Empath — "Help me feel like myself again."
- Seeks emotional reassurance & multi-symptom relief
- Reads reviews that describe lived experience
- **Triggers:** Warmth, inclusivity, relatable storytelling, clear time-to-feel expectations
- **Design guidance:** Warm yet restrained visuals, human tone, avoid clinical stiffness or over-promising

### 3. The Pragmatist — "Keep it simple and worth it."
- Decision efficiency; wants clear function & value
- Scans bullets, price per serving, Subscribe & Save
- **Triggers:** Concise benefit bullets, clear timelines, obvious value & directions
- **Design guidance:** Structured layouts, clear icons for benefits, no fluff, one-line benefit bullets

### 4. The Minimalist — "Show me calm confidence."
- Judges in seconds; equates simplicity with quality
- Reads only headlines and callouts
- **Triggers:** Whitespace, consistency, restraint, few meaningful words
- **Design guidance:** Neutral palette, modern sans-serif type, minimalist layout, quiet visual language, concise headlines

## Persona Integration Matrix

| Persona | Role in Communication |
|---------|----------------------|
| Researcher | Transparent proof (ingredient rationale, dosage clarity) |
| Empath | Warm headlines & relatable testimonials |
| Pragmatist | Concise benefit bullets & time-to-relief |
| Minimalist | Whitespace & visual restraint |

All four inform tone, packaging, and website/PDP layout.

## Consumer Journey (Discovery → Loyalty)

1. **Discovery:** Head (ingredients) vs Heart (stories) vs Ease (value & clarity) → first visual impression decides click-through
2. **Evaluation:** Facts & reviews vs relatable relief narratives
3. **Decision:** Credible proof + simple promise + aesthetic trust
4. **Experience:** Daily habit; time-to-feel; low friction
5. **Advocacy:** Detailed reviews (Researcher), shared stories (Empath), S&S loyalty (Pragmatist), display-worthy packaging (Minimalist)
